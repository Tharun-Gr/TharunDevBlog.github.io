Executive Summary

Developed an EdTech platform targeting early childhood development, with distinct applications for parents, educational partners, and cohort-based learning. Employing Docker and Kubernetes for scalability, the platform facilitates enhanced parental engagement through educational content and community interaction, aligned with the TMW Center's 3Ts program principles.

Project Overview

Focused on empowering parents of infants to 3-year-olds, the platform provides tools and content to promote critical early brain development practices through three specialized applications: Partner App, Parent App, and Cohort App.

Partner App

Enables educational partners to onboard parents and curate custom educational content, directly influencing the early learning journey.

Parent App

Delivers personalized content to parents, structured into 10 interactive levels with gamification elements to encourage engagement and learning.

Cohort App

Supports dual modes for educators and parents, facilitating cohort-based learning sessions and community engagement.

Technical Implementation

Utilizes Docker and Kubernetes for robust system performance and scalability. Features include a bespoke video player for parent lectures and an appointment management module for partner collaborations.

Impact and Future Directions

The platform significantly contributes to early childhood education by enhancing parental involvement and providing educational partners with effective tools. Future enhancements will aim to expand content and interactivity, supporting broader 3Ts program goals.

Conclusion

This EdTech platform project marks a significant advancement in supporting early childhood development through technology, demonstrating a commitment to educational excellence and the well-being of future generations.
