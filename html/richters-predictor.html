Objective

This project explores the effectiveness of various machine learning models, with a focus on ensemble learning, to predict earthquake-induced damage to buildings. Inspired by the "Richter’s Predictor" research, the aim was to find the most efficient model for assessing damage severity caused by earthquakes.

Data and Methods

Utilizing a dataset with details on building structures and socio-economic factors from the Gorkha earthquake, the study examined multiple models including Logistic Regression, KNN, Random Forest, and XGBoost. Techniques such as feature selection and parameter tuning were employed, alongside sampling methods to address data imbalance.

Findings

XGBoost Excellence

XGBoost outperformed other models and ensemble methods in predicting damage grades, showcasing high accuracy and F1 score.

Ensemble Learning Insights

While combining models (KNN, Random Forest, and XGBoost) into ensembles slightly improved predictions, XGBoost alone was more efficient, challenging the notion that more complex models always yield better results.

Impact of Sampling

Sampling techniques aimed at balancing the dataset showed mixed effectiveness, underscoring the complexity of managing class imbalance in machine learning.

Conclusion

The project underscores XGBoost's robustness in earthquake damage prediction, offering valuable insights for disaster management strategies. It opens pathways for future research to enhance predictive modeling in natural disaster contexts, emphasizing the potential of machine learning in real-world applications.
